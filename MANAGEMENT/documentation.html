Harvard Buisness Review

Time after time, we observed that once a manager had decided to make the move and incorporate a blind person into his or her work group, the climate there became a very positive one for the new employee. The failures were always due to factors other than vision limitations, factors that can disrupt any employer-worker relationship. Yet even in those cases, the work group often felt a strong desire to recruit another visually impaired or blind person. They had an investment in the cause.

General Problems

Here's some of the big problems we noted

Pronunciation of "jargon" is a major problem.

Screen-readers are not well-equiped to read the math in its intended order of operations, nor pronounce it correctly

Braille is a solution but also a problem if the teacher does not know Braille

It's very difficult for the student and teacher to have a timely, two-way conversation if both teacher and student do not know Braille. There are mercifully some software programs, like Braille Blaster, that help, but it is not the most direct line of communication.

Terminlogy Notes
Accessibility Support: Level of Assistance Accessibility Support is not a one-size-fits-all solution. Context figures prominently. According to WCAG, This is a complex topic and one that varies both by environment and by language as accessed 9 March 2020
Accessibility Supported: WCAG 2.0 Definition Using a technology in a way that is accessibility supported means that it works with assistive technologies (AT) and the accessibility features of operating systems, browsers, and other user agents. Technology features can only be relied upon to conform to WCAG 2.0 success criteria if they are used in a way that is "accessibility supported". Technology features can be used in ways that are not accessibility supported (do not work with assistive technologies, etc.) as long as they are not relied upon to conform to any success criterion (i.e., the same information or functionality is also available another way that is supported). Important Terms in WCAG 2.0 as accessed 9 March 2020
