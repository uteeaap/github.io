General Solutions

In general, tactile and audio versions of important concepts in Art can go far to giving a blind or low-vision student a way of learning about important concepts in Art. For those with mobility problems, it's surprising how "everyday" tools can extend the artist's body in order to create Art.

Creation as Lifeblood

Technology goes far in enabling a blind man to perceive the color blue (through sound). Technology enables a scultpor who is unable to walk to produce giant steel sculptures. It enables blind visitors to enjoy a museum vist through touch.

Images in Relief

Using three-dimensional, raised edges on an images -- tactile images -- are an almost universal solution to allowing the blind to "see" images. This is particularly good for representing two-dimensional images. There's three distinct areas in which relief image can help a teacher extend Art education to those with disabilities. First, as a "creator", the technology allows a teacher to rapidly create a tactile image that would otherwise be extremely time-consuming. Second, as a "passive" skill, a student can learn about an image by touch. Finally, as an active skill, a blind student can take that tactile image and produce great Art with it.
