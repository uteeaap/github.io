Why Write Music?

Althoug the musical ear is a great help, for the serious study of music the ability to read and write musical notation is essential

And this is just as try for the blind student as it is for the sighted

Writing Braille Music (YouTube Video) | Music4VIP

For accreditation purposes, there is a requirement to be able to read and write music. Because of technology, writing music is largely accessible to those with disabilities, because of music-writing technology that has become widely accepted in the field; in particular, Sibelius, and Finale. Reading music is more problematic. Neither Braille nor LaTeX, two commonly promoted solutions, do not offer the same opportunity to perceive music notation holistically, which is necessary for discipline-specific discussions of music. The function of a chord, how measures are outlined, the position of the staff and where it rests all must be processed holistically. Linear processes such as those used in Braille and LaTeX do not allow for this. Consequently, should a blind student with to pursue advanced music, tactical means will be used, such as raised paper or three-dimensional representations of the music.
