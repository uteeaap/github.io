Multiple challenges

And they are all worth the fight. Nursing requires an ability to understand the logic of math as well as the complexity of human anatomy

For the many hand-written Math problems

Anyone who can write by hand, can write on this tablet, and put the Math in a form that can be made accessible.

There's a significant learning curve to using some of the accessibility software associated with Math. Therefore, one way around the problem is to use software that captures the handwriting and is able to convert the handwriting to a digital format. From the digital format, accessible versions of math may be created.

Works Well

We procured the Zytex2 and found it to work surpringly well. We were able to reproduce math graphs easily on a laser printer. Complicated graphs may be reproduced fairly easily, although the grid-lines themselves probably require extra attention. Remember that the text will need to be in Braille, but you can use Braille Blaster to do that!

For more information, see the YouTube channel for American Printing House for the Blind's pages on BrailleBlaster. We've included some of their videos on the Resources page.

WARWICK, R.I. (WPRI) — A local doctor made it over a life-changing hurdle, then kept running toward an “ice cream dream.” Two days before Doctor Ray Mis turned 40, his world suddenly went dark. “All I had was peripheral vision,” Mis said from his Warwick office. Unexplained optic nerve atrophy made him legally blind. As a gastroenterologist, if he can’t see the issue, he can’t treat it. His career seemed over. 'It was more disbelief and shock and it turned into, ‘this can’t be happening to me,'

But he tracked down technology to help him read vital information from procedures like colonoscopies.
